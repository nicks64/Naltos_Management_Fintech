# ✅ **THE SIMPLE, REASONABLE FLOW OF MONEY (Naltos Model)**

## **1. Tenant → Naltos (Rent Payment Inbound)**

**Tenants pay rent through Naltos** using ACH/card/stablecoin.
Funds land in a **custodial account (FDIC-insured)** or in a **1:1 backed stablecoin (NUSD)**.

**This is your “float”—your raw material.**

---

## **2. Naltos Treasury → Short-Term Yield Vehicle**

For 5–15 days (until owner payout), Naltos moves idle rent funds into ultra-safe, short-term instruments:

* **Tokenized T-bills**
* **Money-market style instruments**
* Optional: **Delta-neutral BTC-backed notes** (low exposure, hedged)

**These generate 4–6% APY.**

This is your **yield engine**.

---

## **3. Yield Accrues Daily**

While rent is sitting, yield accumulates.
Example:

* $1M rent × (10/365 days) × 5.5% = **$1,507**
* This happens every month.

This is where **owners earn more NOI**, tenants get incentives, and Naltos earns margin.

---

## **4. Naltos → Owner Payouts (Outflow)**

Rent is disbursed to property owners on their chosen schedule (daily/weekly/monthly).

* Owners always receive **100% of their rent principal.**
* Yield is distributed separately according to your split.

**This keeps everything clean and compliant.**

---

## **5. Yield Distribution**

Yield is split like this (example):

| Receiver             | Share        | Purpose                   |
| -------------------- | ------------ | ------------------------- |
| Owners               | **2.5–3.5%** | Boost NOI                 |
| Tenants              | **1–1.5%**   | On-time payment incentive |
| Naltos (your profit) | **0.5–1%**   | Treasury + platform fee   |

This is the **business model**.

---

## **6. Vendor Payments (Optional Layer)**

If property managers have **Net 30 / Net 45** agreements:

* rent float lasts longer
* more yield is earned
* owner gains more NOI
* Naltos earns higher treasury margin

This is how **payables timing increases your revenue** without any extra cost.

# 📌 **In One Clear Sentence**

> **Tenants pay rent → Naltos holds the funds temporarily → deploys the float into short-term, low-risk yield → pays owners and vendors → shares part of the yield with tenants and owners → keeps a small margin.**
