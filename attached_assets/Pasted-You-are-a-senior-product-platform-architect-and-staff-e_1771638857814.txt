You are a senior product + platform architect and staff engineer. 
Design a production-ready, compliance-aware, multi-tenant SaaS platform for multifamily “rent + incentives + savings + payments + marketplace” with multiple portals and multiple user types (operators, tenants, vendors, merchants, partners). 

GOAL
Produce a complete implementation-grade blueprint: requirements, user roles, auth/permissions, workflows, data model, APIs, system architecture, compliance constraints, audit controls, and a phased delivery plan. Assume the platform must scale from pilot → national scale.

CONTEXT
This platform serves multifamily owners/operators and their tenants.
Core wedge: “Turn rent into incentives + savings outcomes.” 
Over time: enable tenants to transact with merchants (marketplace), and enable vendors (multifamily service providers) to operate through a vendor portal.

CRITICAL REQUIREMENT
There will be multiple types of userbases and authentications:
- Operators (ownership groups, property managers)
- Tenants (renters)
- Vendors (service providers who work with the property: plumbers, HVAC, cleaners, landscapers, security, pest control, contractors, etc.)
- Merchants (retail/service businesses tenants purchase from, potentially offering offers/cashback)
- Internal admins and support
- Partners (insurance providers, mortgage partners, investment vehicle partners, etc.)
Design portal separation, authentication, and authorization carefully across these groups.

ASSUMPTIONS
- Multi-tenant SaaS: many operator organizations; each org has properties, units, tenants.
- Must support role-based access control (RBAC) and optionally attribute-based access control (ABAC) for fine-grained permissions.
- Must support SSO for operators/vendors/merchants where needed (SAML/OIDC), and passwordless for tenants optional.
- Must support audit logs, event logs, and strong data separation between organizations.
- Must be compliant-aware: avoid providing regulated services directly; integrate via licensed/regulated partners when needed.

SPECIAL DESIGN RULES
- Any feature that touches funds, yield, investment, or insurance must be partner-led unless there is a licensing plan.
- For tenant “savings”: treat as a ledger + partner-managed account. Platform should not custody funds unless compliant.
- Every action that can affect money or access must generate an audit log record.
- Separate “identity” from “profile” so a user can have multiple personas (tenant + vendor owner + operator employee).
- Include a portal-level boundary: a vendor should not see any other property’s data unless invited/assigned.
- Merchants should only see their own transactions/offers and aggregated insights, not tenant personal data unless consented.
- Include dispute workflows: rent disputes, merchant disputes, vendor invoice disputes.
- Include “consent capture” and “data sharing preferences” for tenants.

TECH STACK PREFERENCES
Recommend a pragmatic stack:
- Backend: TypeScript (NestJS/Express) or Python (FastAPI) + Postgres
- Auth: Auth0/Cognito/Clerk/Keycloak (recommend one with enterprise SSO)
- Payments: Stripe, Adyen, or property payment rails integration (abstract)
- Eventing: SQS/Kafka/PubSub, plus outbox pattern
- Infra: AWS or GCP, containerized, IaC (Terraform)
But you may propose alternatives with rationale.
