# **📄 NALTOS — OPTIMAL CASH FLOW ARCHITECTURE (Updated with “Stablecoins = Rails”)**

Stablecoins are **payment rails and transport infrastructure** — fast, global, programmable. They **do not generate yield**.

Naltos uses these rails (USDC, USDT, DAI, etc.) to move money instantly and programmatically.
The **treasury engine** (NRF / NRK / NRC) generates the actual yield.
NUSD acts as the **programmable internal dollar** that lets the treasury automate distribution, incentives, and settlement.

Below is the updated, complete cash flow lifecycle across **multifamily → vendors → merchants → tenants → P2P**.

# **2. GLOBAL FLOW OF DOLLARS (All Stakeholders)**

### **USD → Stablecoin Rails → NUSD → Treasury → Yield → Redemption**

This applies to:

* Rent
* Vendor invoices
* Merchant payments
* Tenant rewards
* Tenant P2P
* Owner payouts
* Merchant settlement
* Vendor redemptions

Every dollar follows the same logic:

```text
USD enters → converted to stablecoins (USDC/USDT/DAI)
↓
Stablecoins are rails → used to mint NUSD
↓
NUSD enters one of 4 Float Buckets (0–3, 5–15, 30–90, 1–3 days)
↓
Each bucket allocated to treasury products:
   NRF = T-bills
   NRK = Money market
   NRC = Delta-neutral credit
↓
Yield generated
↓
Yield distributed to stakeholders (owners, tenants, vendors, platform)
↓
On payout → NUSD redeemed → stablecoins → USD → bank/card/chain
```

This is the universal model — now broken out by stakeholder type.

# **3. MULTIFAMILY RENT FLOW (5–15 Days)**

**Stablecoins are rails.
NUSD is the programmable dollar.
NRF/NRK generate yield.**

### **Step-by-step:**

1. Tenant pays rent through AppFolio / Buildium / Yardi.
2. PMS sends USD to property bank.
3. Naltos uses stablecoin rails (USDC/USDT/DAI) to mint **1:1 NUSD**.
4. Rent enters **Rent Float Bucket (5–15 days)**.
5. Allocation:

* **70–80% → NRF (T-bills)**
* **20–30% → NRK (money-market)**

6. Yield generated → typically 3–4% APY.
7. Distributed:

* **Owner: 90%**
* **Platform: 10%**
* **Tenant: Cashback bonus (from platform share)**

8. On disbursement day:

* NUSD → stablecoins → USD
* Owner receives full rent + yield

### **Why this works:**

Rent is predictable, short-duration float → perfect for safe, short-term yield.

---

# **4. VENDOR INVOICE FLOW (Net30–Net90)**

**Stablecoins are instant rails for vendor payouts.
Float window (30–90 days) → highest yield engine.**

### **Step-by-step:**

1. Vendor issues invoice ($10,000, Net30).
2. Property manager approves in PMS.
3. Naltos pays vendor **instantly in NUSD** (Day 0).
4. Vendor sees funds immediately in Vendor Portal.
5. Naltos holds USD equivalent for **30–90 days** using stablecoin rails.
6. Allocation (higher yield because longer duration):

* **50% → NRF**
* **30% → NRK**
* **20% → NRC** (delta-neutral credit)

7. Yield distributed:

* Owner: **90%**
* Vendor: **5% cashback**
* Platform: **5%**

8. On day 30/60/90:

* Vendor redeems NUSD → USD via ACH or instant card/chain rails.

### **Why this works:**

Vendors get instant liquidity.
Owners monetize the float period.
Naltos earns yield without operational risk.

---

# **5. MERCHANT SETTLEMENT FLOW (1–3 Days)**

**Stablecoins = instant rails for tenant → merchant.
Treasury engine earns micro-yield during settlement window.**

### **Step-by-step:**

1. Tenant spends $50 at a merchant using NUSD.
2. Merchant portal reflects new NUSD balance.
3. Merchant sets settlement preferences (daily/2-day/weekly).
4. Treasury holds merchant balances in a **Micro Float Bucket (1–3 days)**.
5. Allocation:

* **50–70% → NRK (money market)**
* **30–50% → liquidity buffer**

6. Yield distribution:

* Owner (50%)
* Tenant cashback (40%)
* Platform (10%)

### **Why this works:**

High volume of low-duration transactions produce predictable, compounding float.

# **6. TENANT P2P (Closed Community Economy)**

**Stablecoins = rails.
NUSD = the community currency.
Float = yield while NUSD circulates.**

### Flow:

1. Tenant sends NUSD → roommate, neighbor, or friend.
2. Funds move instantly (stablecoin rails).
3. Treasury holds P2P balances in **Global Liquidity + Micro Float Bucket**.
4. No conversion, no ACH, no delays.
5. More NUSD = more float = more treasury deployment.
6. Yield is generated indirectly:

* NUSD balances sitting idle
* Settlement buffers
* Liquidity reserves

### Why this matters:

* Tenants keep NUSD in the ecosystem.
* Merchant spend increases.
* Owners indirectly benefit from network-wide float.
* Naltos becomes the **financial layer of the community**.
